About Us



The biggest questions that so many people as are, who are the Greek Gods? Why are there so many of them?
What do they represent? On this page, those questions will be answered. Well, mostly, anyways.

Who are the Greek Gods?

The first question is probably the biggest and ties the other two together. Who are the Greek Gods?
The Greek Gods are deities that have been around in the Greek culture for many, many years.
The Greek Gods were first created to try and explain natural and non natural phenomenen that
were happening around the world at the time. This is where the myths come from.
Many people still worship these gods and goddesses today, though for different reasons and in differnent
ways than they used to.

Who are the main Greek Gods?

Although there are many different Greek deities all throughout the Greek Pantheon, the most popular
and therefore the most powerful, deities are the main 13: Zeus, Poseidon, Hera, Demeter, Athena,
Apollo, Artemis, Ares, Aphrodite, Hephaestus, Hermes, Hades and Dionysus.
Many people also know of two very important goddesses, Hestia and Persephone, but they are
not featured in the main 13 gods as they are a little less popular.

Zeus

Zeus, the king of the gods and the highest authority on Mount Olympus is probably the most well-known
god in the Greek Pantheon. His powers are over the sky and the weather and is idential to the Roman god,
Jupiter. Zeus has been hailed as the bringer of thunder, lightning, rain and winds for centuries and he
weilds a powerful lightning bolt that he hurls at his enemies to cut them down. He has been viewed as the
father of both the human race and many gods and demi-gods throughout history. Zeus was the last of the
orginal gods to be born and, with the help of his mother, Rhea, killed is father, Cronos, with is own scythe,
chopped him up into bits and threw him into Tartarus, the deepest pit of the Underwold. After overthrowing
their father, Zeus and his siblings Poseidon, Hades, Hera, Demeter, and Hestia banished the Titans
(offspring of Gaea and Uranus) into the underworld for all eternity. He then presided over Mount Olympus,
the home of the gods, and he and his brothers drew lots to see what domain they would rule. Zeus got
the sky, Poseidon got the sea and Hades got the Underworld.

Zeus later married Hera (yes, he married his sister, but that's pretty much par for the course for greek
mythology), but he wasn't always faithful. He had love affairs with both mortal and immortal women, although
some of his most famous consorts would have to be Leda, Europa, Leto, Demeter (again, yes, his own sister)
the Titaness Metis, and the goddess Semele although there were many others. Each time he would attempt to seduce another
woman, he would turn into a different creature form like a cuckoo, or a swan, or a bull, and then would turn back into
himself. Hera always knew about each of them and would curse each and every one of Zeus's constorts.
