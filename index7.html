The biggest questions that so many people as are, who are the Norse Gods? Why are there so many of them?
What do they represent? On this page, those questions will be answered. Well, mostly, anyways.

Who are the Norse Gods?

The first question is probably the biggest and ties the other two together. Who are the Norse Gods?
The Norse Gods are deities that have been around in the Norse culture for many, many years.
The Nrse Gods were first created to try and explain natural and non natural phenomenen that
were happening around the world at the time. This is where the myths come from.
Many people still worship these gods and goddesses today, though for different reasons and in differnent
ways than they used to.

Who are the main Norse Gods?

Although there are many different Norse deities all throughout the Norse Pantheon, the most popular
and therefore the most powerful, deities are the main 12: Odin, Frigg, Thor, Loki, Freya, Balder,
Hel, Tyr, Njoror, Freyr, Heimdall, and Hermoor.
Many people also know of two very important gods, Vidar and Idunn, but they are
not featured in the main 12 gods as they are a little less popular.
